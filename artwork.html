Artist and Model

Artist and Model' is based on a scene of fellow artist Chen Wen Hsi sketching a Balinese woman, during their Bali expedition in 1952 together with Cheong Soo Pieng and Chen Chong Swee.




Chinese Bridge over River

This painting shows a multiple arch bridge topped with a Chinese roof; in the far distance are lofty mountains. A preparatory sketch for this painting, dated 1974, is in the museum’s collection.




Fisherman at Seaside

Liu Kang often travelled within and outside Singapore, and as he did so, he painted the places he visited.





Life by the River

It depicts a bucolic scene of daily life in a traditional kampung or village in 1970s Singapore. The background features lush tropical greenery and a group of typical Malay wooden kampung houses on stilts, or rumah panggung, with gabled roofs.




Near the seaside

Although Liu Kang was trained as an oil painter, he used pastel for portraiture and later employed it for other genres such as still life and landscape, eventually earning a reputation as one of the best pastel artists in the Southeast Asia.




Scenery

Scenery this artwork is created when Liu kang travelled within and outside Singapore to seek subjects for his art. With brilliant colours, Liu Kang renders a large driftwood on a shore.





Batik Workers

The historic trip to Bali in 1952 introduced many new sights and experiences to Liu Kang and his fellow artists, and provided a wealth of new subject matter for their art. This sketch shows ladies applying wax on cloth in a batik factory.




Construction Site

From the 1940s to 1950s, Liu Kang travelled widely in Malaya to paint the people, their way of life and the landscape. Rebuilding was everywhere in post-war Malaya and here, Liu Kang depicts labourers hard at work at a construction site.




Life in a Village

The island of Bali with its rich and exotic culture was a source of inspiration for Liu Kang and his fellow artists. It provided them with new subject matter and was crucial to the development of the Nanyang style. This undated pastel work is probably drawn on one of Liu Kang trips there.





Mt.Huangshan

In the 1980s, Liu Kang made several trips to Huangshan in Anhui, China, a mountain range famous for its scenic views and a source of inspiration for countless Chinese poets and artists.He had also travelled there with his teacher, Liu Haisu.




Painting Kampong

In the 1950s, Liu Kang experimented with the technique of batik painting with his friend and fellow artist, Chuah Thean Teng. Although painted in oils, the influence of batik is apparent in the white outlines, large areas of single flat colour and the elimination of small details.




Picnic

In the 1950s, Liu Kang experimented with the technique of batik painting with his friend and fellow artist, Chuah Thean Teng. Although painted in oil, ‘Picnic’ shows the influence of batik — the white outlines being reminiscent of undyed areas of the cloth.
