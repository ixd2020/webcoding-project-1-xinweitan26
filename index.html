Welcome to Liu Kang website

Liu Kang (Chinese: 刘抗; pinyin: Liú Kàng) was a Singaporean artist known for his Balinese-themed figurative paintings.

He was a founding member of the Singapore Art Society, and was credited with developing the Nanyang Style, an art style associated with the Nanyang Academy of Fine Arts.[1] He is also the father of the architect Liu Thai Ker.

Liu was born in Fujian Province and he spent his early years in Malaysia,
studied art in Shanghai and Paris, and taught art in Shanghai during the 1930s.
Under the influence of Chinese artist and art teacher Liu Haisu (1896–1994), Liu admired, and often appropriated the styles of French-based modernist painters such as Cézanne, van Gogh and Matisse.
